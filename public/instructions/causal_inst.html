Overview

You'll be asked to find the event(s) that is the reason another event have happened. To do so, you'll be given events that started (in time) before the event in question. You need to decide if one of those events was/is the reason. For example,

[The hurricane hit the Florida coast causing severe property damage.]

[QUESTION] Why (damage) had (have/will) happened?

Because of:

EXPLANATION: (damage) happened because the hurricane (hit) Florida coast, this case is rather simple as it is indicated explicitly in the sentence by the word "causing".

IMPORTANT RULE 1

Look for a clear indication in the context (i.e., causation words such as: cause, because, incite, influence, inspire, lead, prompt, push, force, make, set, start, trigger and so on). Such indicator can help make the decision when they exist and indicate one event caused the other.

However, Not all cases are a clear-cut as (hit) and (damage), in many cases, there will be no clear indication in the sentence. For example,

[He loaded the gun, shot and killed the attacker.]

[QUESTION] Why (killed) had (have/will) happened?

Because of:

EXPLANATION: Here we need to use a bit of common-sense to figure that (killed) happened because of (shot).

IMPORTANT RULE 2

Given an event in context, ask why the event happened, what triggered the event or caused it to happened. For example "why the attacker was killed?. We can search if the answer is one of the events in the list."

However, events are not always triggered by other events, for example:

[She arrived home, connected to the Wifi and logged in to her email account.]

[QUESTION] Why (logged) had (have/will) happened?

Because of:

EXPLANATION: (logged) didn't happened because she (arrived) or (connected). While the connected event enabled her to log into her account, if we ask "why she logged into her email account?" a reasonable answer will be that she wanted to check her emails rather then because she connected to the WiFi. Events that enable the event in question to happened should not be considered as the causing events.
